Hello, everyone!

My name is Jessy, and I study International Relations with Quantitative Methods. As a school representative, my purpose will be to improve communication between students and staff in order to gain better representation of the students’ views (including less vocal ones). I plan to do this by organising monthly meetings with class representatives and creating an anonymous online feedback form.   more

For this term I will focus on two main issues:

  1. Improve submission deadline dates:
    • Popular outside modules such as Social Anthropology have the same deadlines as compulsory courses.
    • I hope to spread out deadlines for the most popular and frequent courses within the school.
    • I believe that if the course organisers from SPS were to cooperate, we can improve deadline dates, even if the deadlines are two days apart.
  2. Closing the feedback loop:
    • To achieve this goal, we should make the feedback process efficient and fundamental.
    • This has been on last year’s agenda and despite all the efficient feedbacks given, I would like to emphasise the importance of having constructive and timely feedback, i.e. promoting more uniform electronic remarks & making sure that tutors advertise their availability after an assignment has been returned.

Other issues that I wish to explore include:

  • Keeping an eye on the “Lecture Recording Project” and help to implement it smoothly when it reaches our school.
  • Communication with other schools.
  • Finding out the general opinion about the dictionary policy and its effect on students and take action if needed
  • Make sure that essays are due after the EUSA peer proofreading session for second language students who wish to use the service.
